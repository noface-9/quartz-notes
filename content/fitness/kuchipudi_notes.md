## Natyarambha Sloka

```
praṇamya śhirasā dewau – Bowing my head in reverence to the two deities
pitāmaha-maheśhvarau – Grandfather Brahma and Lord Shiva
nāṭyaśhāstraṁ pravakṣhyāmi – I shall now explain the Nāṭyaśāstra
brahmaṇā yat daśāhṛtam (dhutahrutham)– As it was originally given (or sent down) by Brahma

devatānāṁ śhirasthaḥ tu – (The hands) should be placed above the head when saluting the gods.
gurūṇām asya saṁsthitaḥ – At the forehead level when saluting gurus (teachers).
vakṣhasthaḥ cha eva viprāṇām – At the chest level when saluting Brahmanas (learned ones).
śheṣaḥ tu niyamaḥ bhavet – This is the prescribed rule of salutation.

viṣhṇu-śhakti-samutpanne – O one born from the power (śakti) of Vishnu
citra-varṇe mahī-tale – O beautifully colored one, O surface of the earth
anekaratna-sampanne – Rich with countless gems and treasures
bhūmi-devī namo’stu te – O Bhūmi Devī, my salutations to you

samudra-vāsanām devīm – The goddess clothed in oceans (i.e., the Earth surrounded by seas)
parvata-sthana-maṇḍalām – Whose bosom is adorned with mountains
nāṭyaṁ kariṣye bhū-devīm – I now begin dance, O Goddess Bhudevi
padāghātaṁ kṣhamasva me – Please forgive the stamping of my feet

kaṭi-karṇa-samāyātram – Alignment from the waist to the ears
kūrpara-aṁśa-śhiras tathā – With elbows, shoulders, and head properly positioned
samunnatam uras cha eva – With the chest lifted and elevated
sauṣhṭavam nāma tat bhavet – This is called sauṣṭhavam (a posture of grace, strength, and symmetry)

prāyeṇa kāraṇe kāryaḥ vāmavakṣhasthitaḥ karaḥ
– In general, for expressive or causal gestures (kāraṇa), the left hand should be placed near the left side of the chest.
charaṇasya anugaḥ cha eva dakṣiṇaḥ tu bhavet karaḥ
– The right hand should follow the movement of the foot (as a supportive or following gesture).
```
