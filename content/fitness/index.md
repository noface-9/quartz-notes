## What is this?

My fitness journey began in 2022, though it looked nothing like it does today. At first, it was simply ten minutes of exercise each morning, along with the walks to and from college or through the park. It wasn't about building muscle or chasing a particular physique—it was about slowly learning to care for my body.

Before that, I had given very little thought to my health. I took my body and its vitality for granted. Chips and samosas were part of my daily routine, I frequently skipped proper meals, and junk food made up a significant portion of my diet. Over time, the consequences caught up with me: dull, dehydrated skin, hair fall, vitamin deficiencies, constant fatigue, low motivation, and declining mental well-being.

Then came a moment that quietly changed everything.

In 2022, I spent three days alone in my room with almost no contact with anyone. I passed the time endlessly scrolling on my phone, staring at the ceiling, and barely eating. On the fourth morning, I woke up, got ready, stepped outside into the sunlight, and simply paused. As I stood there, I became acutely aware of how I felt—physically and mentally. In that moment, I had a thought:

> *This isn't what I'm here for. I don't want to spend my life merely existing.*

That realization didn't transform my life overnight, but it changed its direction.

I committed just ten minutes every morning to moving my body in the sunlight. Those ten minutes became the foundation of a habit that has stayed with me ever since.

If someone looked at me today, they might not notice a dramatic physical transformation. But I do. My skin glows, my hair is healthier and stronger, I have energy throughout the day, and perhaps most importantly, I've developed a much deeper appreciation for the body that carries me through life.
