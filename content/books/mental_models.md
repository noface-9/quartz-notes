## Vol 1: General Thinking Concepts

> The key to better understanding the world is to build a latticework of mental models. 

*You only think you know, as a matter of fact. And most of your actions are based on incomplete knowledge and you really don't know what it is all about, or what the purpose of the world is, or know a great deal of other things. It is possible to live and not know.*

### 1. Acquiring wisdom

> I believe in the discipline of mastering the best of what other people have figured out. 

- All of us have **subconscious mental models** which affect the way we think and reason, infer causality, match patterns, and draw analogies. 
- To see a problem for what it is, we must first **break it down to substantive parts** so the interconnections are clear. 
- **When understanding is separated from reality - we lose powers** (just like Antaeus lost to Heracles after losing contact with his mother, Earth)
- Everything I learn **must be tested** against reality & updated accordingly. Continuous process.
- It's hard to understand a system that we are part of because we have **blind spots**.

##### How do we fail to update even after interacting with reality? 
1. Not having the right perspective or vantage point - hard time seeing any system that we are in (dropping a ball in a moving ship - vertical and horizontal change in position). 
2. Ego-induced denial - invested in our opinions of ourselves to see the world's feedback (to update our beliefs about reality)
    * Afraid about what others will say about us - we don't put our ideas out there and subject them to criticism. 
    * If we do put our ideas out there and they are criticized - our ego steps in to protect us. 
    * We become invested in defending instead of upgrading our ideas.
3. Distance from consequences of our decisions - easier to keep our current views rather than update them. 
    * Further we are from the results/feedback of our decisions, easier to convince ourselves that we are right and avoid the challenge, the pain of updating our views. 

> A man who has committed a mistake and doesn't correct it, is committing another mistake. 

> Most geniuses - especially those who lead others - prosper not by deconstructing intricate complexities but by exploiting unrecognized simplicities. 

We not only understand the immediate problem with more accuracy, but we can begin to see the second-, third-, and higher-order consequences. 

##### Three buckets of Knowledge: 
1. Inorganic Systems - Laws of math and physics, the entire physical universe
2. Organic Systems - Biology on Earth
3. Human History 

##### Explaining Blind Spots
Think of a forest

> Well, the first rule is that you can't really know anything if you just remember isolated facts and try and bang'em back. If the facts don't hang together on a latticework of theory, you don't have them in usable form. You've got to have models in your head. Have to array your experience both vicarious and direct on this latticework on models.

Using and failing, as long as I acknowledge, reflect, and learn from it, is also how I can build my repertoire.

### 2. The Map is not the territory

**The description of the thing is not the thing itself**

##### Features of a Map
1. Maps have a structure similar or dissimilar to the territory - they desribe in a *useful* way with *specific purpose*. Not everything to everyone. 
2. Similar structures have similary "logical" characteristics. 
3. Map is not the actual territory. Can't experience how it's like in the territory as it is. Nor can be used for another purpose.
4. Ideal map - will have another map to explain it, which will have another map. Then you'd never have any issues but eventually it's overwhelming. 

News is an abstraction created by other people. Models and abstractions fail to understand their limits. 
Problem ‼️ - If our knowledge becomes of the map, rather than actual underlying territory it describes. 
We mistake map for reality and think we have all the answers, create static rules or policies that deal with the map - but forget we exist in a changing world. 
**We have to udnerstand the limitations of maps & models**

##### Important considerations to use a map accurately:
1. Reality is the ultimate update - feedback loops by the explorers
2. Consider the Cartographer - Not objective - reflects the values, standards & limitations of their creators (Western influence in modern maps)
3. Maps can influence territories - Elaborate models of design without paying attention to how things work in reality

### 3. Circle of Competence

> I'm no genius. I'm smart in spots - but I stay around those spots

When ego and not competence drives what we undertake, we have blind spots. 

##### Questions answered:
1. What is a circle of competence? Lifer vs Stranger concept. 
2. How do you know when you have one? We know exactly what we don't know. Can make quick and relatively accurate decisions. Know what is knowable, unknowable & can distinguish between both. 
3. How do you build and maintain one?
4. How do you operate outside of one?
